Explore LA!

The South Bay

{% easy_map "South Bay, Los Angeles County, CA" 100 100 10 using 'wedding/blocks/explore_map.html'%}

With the wedding in Palos Verdes, you'll likely be staying in or near Los Angeles's South Bay which notably includes Hermosa, Manhattan and Redondo Beaches. There are fun areas around Hermosa and Redondo Beach piers, as well as many great hiking/outdoor opportunities if you're so inclined.

The Torrance area is well-known for its Japanese Food, and some of Dan's favorite 小笼包 (xiaolongbao, shanghai-style soup dumplings) can be found at Din Tai Fung in the Del Amo Fashion Center.

Nearby Terranea resort has an award-winning 9-hole golf course which can also be a fun activity for a cool February afternoon. The resort also has many other activities including a spa and pools.

Whichever activites you choose, please be sure to not do anything that lands you at Jennifer's Hospital,Harbor-UCLA, in Torrance :). She'll be busy with wedding festivities, though her talented colleagues will assuredly take care of you if need be.

Downtown

{% easy_map "111 S Grand Ave, Los Angeles, CA 90012" 100 100 10 using 'wedding/blocks/explore_map.html' %}

The heat of Los Angeles is filled with amazing museums that are sure to please all artistic and cultural tastes.

Either before or after the museums it's highly recommended to visit LA's Grand Central Market which is full of food stalls represnting many countries and tastes from around the globe.

The LA philharmonic will be playing a selection of Beethoven, Mozart and Salonen on Saturday aftenoon at 2PM at Walt Disney concert hall.

Interesting neighborhoods to discover are LA's Arts District, Little Tokyo, and Koreatown.

If you're looking for food recommendations, don't hesitate to contact Steven as he and his crew have likely eaten at every restaraunt in LA at this point and can recommend their favorites.

Westside

{% easy_map "Westside, Los Angeles, CA" 100 100 10 using 'wedding/blocks/explore_map.html' %}

LA's Westside area is home to Santa Monica and Venice Beaches as well as Westwood (UCLA), Sawtelle, and pretty much every ritzy area of LA you're familiar with from the movies.

The Getty and Skirball Jewish Cultural Center are two prominent Westside attractions that could fill your entire weekend with activities and attractions. The Getty Villa is also nearby in Malibu if you're looking for a more Roman Classical museum experience.

The Santa Monica Boardwalk ambles along the ocean and is lined with interesting (read: touristy and/or weird) shops. You can head further south along the walk to Muscle Beach to see the bodybuilders if you want to feel terrible about all of the food we've been recommending you eat.

The Sawtelle area has amazing food including a favorite dessert spots, Blockheads Shavery, and a favorite coffee shop Balconi Coffee Company (we highly recommend the almond essence latte).
